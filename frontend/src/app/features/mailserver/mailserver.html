Why all three

DKIM alone is not enough: receiving servers also check SPF (which hosts may send for the domain) and DMARC (what to do when a check fails, and where to send reports). Publish all three to avoid being flagged as spam.

SPF

A single TXT record on the domain apex. mx authorises the hosts listed as MX for the domain; add ip4:, ip6: or include: for any other sender (an SMTP relay, a newsletter provider…). Finish with -all (reject anything else) or ~all (soft fail) while you are still testing. Only one SPF record per domain, and no more than 10 DNS lookups.

DMARC

A TXT record on _dmarc.<domain>. Start with p=none to collect reports without affecting delivery, then move to p=quarantine and finally p=reject once the aggregate reports sent to rua= show that all your legitimate mail passes. adkim=s and aspf=s require strict alignment between the From: domain and the DKIM/SPF domain; use r (relaxed) if you send from subdomains.
